 E-Commerce Sales Dashboard is an interactive Power BI report built to analyze sales, profit, and customer behavior for an e-commerce business. The dashboard consolidates transactional data into a single-page, decision-ready view covering revenue performance, profitability trends, product mix, payment behavior, and geographic distribution :)
Key components:

KPI summary cards — Amount (₹438K), Profit (₹37K), Quantity (6K units), and Average Order Value (₹121K), giving an at-a-glance view of overall business health.
Profit by Month (trend chart) — tracks monthly profitability across the year, highlighting a sustained dip from May through September before recovering sharply in November, surfacing a clear seasonal pattern.
Sum of Quantity by Category (donut chart) — shows Clothing as the dominant category at 63% of units sold, followed by Electronics (21%) and Furniture (17%).
Sum of Quantity by Payment Mode (donut chart) — reveals COD (Cash on Delivery) as the leading payment method at 44%, with UPI, Debit Card, Credit Card, and EMI splitting the remainder — flagging a heavy dependency on a single payment channel.
Sum of Profit by Sub-Category (bar chart) — ranks sub-categories (Printers, Bookcases, Saree, Accessories, Tables) by profit contribution to identify top and underperforming product lines.
Sum of Amount by State (bar chart) — breaks down revenue by geography (Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, Delhi), useful for regional strategy.
Sum of Amount by Customer Name (bar chart) — highlights top customers by revenue contribution.
Quarter slicer — allows interactive filtering of all visuals by Qtr 1–Qtr 4 for time-based drill-down.

Purpose: The dashboard was built to practice translating raw e-commerce transaction data into a structured analytics layer — using SQL for data cleaning/transformation and Power BI/DAX for modeling and visualization — and to extract actionable business insights such as seasonal profit dips and payment-channel concentration risk.
